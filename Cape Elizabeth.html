Cape Elizabeth, Maine

Cape Elizabeth, Maine is a suburb of Portland, the biggest city in Maine. It borders the towns of South Portland to the north and Scarborough to the south. The town has a population of approximately 9,000 in an area of 45.93 square miles. As a coastal town, it is home to multiple beaches and state parks, including Crescent Beach State Park, Two Lights State Park, and Casino Beach at Fort Williams Park. In total, the town has three prestigious lighthouses, with the most famous being Portland Head Light at Fort Williams. There are two more located at Two Lights State Park, but they are privately owned unlike Portland Head Light. Tourists come to Cape Elizabeth by the bus and trolley loads every summer to see all the beauty that the town encompasses. The Beach to Beacon 10K Road Race is run every summer in the town, attracting competitive runners from all around the world to run from Crescent Beach to Portland Head Light at Fort Williams. Cape Elizabeth High School is the only public school in the town, and it is home of the Capers. It has approximately 550 students serving grades 9-12.
